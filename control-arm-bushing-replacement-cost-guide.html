What is a Control Arm Bushing?

A control arm bushing is a rubber or polyurethane component installed between the control arm and chassis. It absorbs vibration and allows controlled movement of the suspension system.

Common Symptoms of Failure

  • Clunking noise when driving over bumps
  • Unstable steering response
  • Uneven tire wear
  • Excess vibration in cabin

Replacement Cost Factors

The cost of replacing control arm bushings depends on several key factors:

  • Vehicle model – luxury cars typically cost more
  • OEM vs aftermarket – OEM parts have higher pricing but better durability
  • Labor cost – varies by region (US/EU vs LATAM vs Africa)
  • Front vs rear suspension complexity
